Acceptable Use Policy

This Acceptable Use Policy establishes the rules of conduct that all WarnApp users must respect. Non-compliance may result in suspension or termination of your account, as well as possible legal action.

1. Fundamental Principles

By using WarnApp, you commit to:

• Act with honesty, integrity, and good faith
• Respect the rights of other users
• Use the service exclusively for its legitimate purposes
• Comply with all applicable laws and regulations
• Not abuse the system or its functionalities
• Contribute to creating a safe and respectful community

2. Strictly Prohibited Conduct

2.1. Illegal or Harmful Content:
• Share pornographic, obscene, or sexually explicit material
• Post content that incites hatred, violence, or discrimination
• Disseminate terrorist content or promote illegal activities
• Share copyrighted material without authorization
• Post content that glorifies self-harm or suicide
• Share material that exploits or harms minors

2.2. Falsehood and Deception:
• Send false or fabricated alerts about vehicles
• Deliberately provide incorrect information
• Register license plates that don't belong to you without authorization
• Create fake accounts or use fictitious identities
• Impersonate another person or entity
• Manipulate photo/video metadata to deceive

2.3. Harassment and Abuse:
• Harass, intimidate, threaten, or abuse other users
• Send multiple unsolicited alerts (spam)
• Stalk other users
• Make personal attacks or insults
• Disclose private information of third parties (doxxing)
• Coordinate attacks against specific users

2.4. Fraud and Unauthorized Commercial Activities:
• Use the platform for scams or phishing
• Sell products or services without authorization
• Conduct unsolicited advertising
• Manipulate the reputation system through fraudulent exchanges
• Create pyramid or multi-level schemes
• Attempt to monetize free features

2.5. Security and Unauthorized Access:
• Attempt to access other users' accounts
• Conduct brute force attacks or hacking attempts
• Distribute malware, viruses, or malicious code
• Exploit security vulnerabilities without reporting them
• Reverse engineer the software
• Use bots, scripts, or unauthorized automation
• Intentionally overload servers (DDoS)

2.6. Privacy and Images:
• Capture and share images of people without their consent
• Photograph or record minors without parental/guardian authorization
• Share images of private properties that invade privacy
• Reveal sensitive locations (residences, schools, hospitals)
• Share personally identifiable data of third parties

3. Responsible Use of Vehicle Alerts

3.1. Alerts MUST:
• Be truthful and based on real facts
• Contain relevant and useful information for the vehicle owner
• Include photographic/video evidence when appropriate
• Be respectful in tone and language
• Protect the privacy of uninvolved third parties
• Focus on the vehicle and situation, not on people

3.2. Alerts MUST NOT:
• Be used for revenge or harassment
• Include insults, threats, or offensive language
• Reveal personal information about the driver
• Be based on assumptions or rumors
• Be repetitive or constitute spam
• Include advertising content

3.3. Appropriate Situations for Alerts:
✓ Lights left on
✓ Windows left open
✓ Flat tires
✓ Recent visible damage
✓ Obstacles or hazards near the vehicle
✓ Parking incidents (badly parked, obstruction)
✓ Cleaning reminders on snowy days
✓ Notices of temporary loading/unloading zones

3.4. Inappropriate Situations:
✗ Personal complaints or neighborhood conflicts
✗ Reports that should be directed to authorities
✗ Criticisms about the vehicle's appearance or maintenance
✗ Information about driver's regular movements
✗ Speculation about the owner

4. Reputation System

The reputation system is essential for maintaining service quality:

4.1. Fair Ratings:
• Base your ratings on the usefulness and truthfulness of the alert
• Don't use negative ratings as a form of revenge
• Be objective and constructive

4.2. Prohibited:
• Coordinate fraudulent ratings
• Create multiple accounts to manipulate scores
• Artificially exchange positive ratings
• Systematically attack a user's reputation

4.3. Consequences of Low Reputation:
• Your alerts may be less visible
• Limits on service functionalities
• Manual review of your account
• Possible suspension if misconduct persists

5. Vehicle Registration

5.1. You can only register license plates of:
• Vehicles you own
• Company vehicles under your responsibility
• Family members' vehicles with express authorization
• Vehicles you are an authorized regular driver of

5.2. It is Prohibited to:
• Register third-party license plates without authorization
• Create false or fabricated registrations
• Impersonate vehicle ownership
• Register vehicles for unauthorized surveillance purposes

5.3. Verification:
• We reserve the right to request proof of ownership/authorization
• We may suspend suspicious registrations
• Falsification of documentation results in permanent expulsion

6. Reports and Complaints

6.1. If You Observe Violations:
• Use the "Report" function on inappropriate alerts
• Provide specific details of the violation
• Do not retaliate or respond with more violations
• Trust our moderation team

6.2. False Reports:
• Malicious or false reports are prohibited
• Systematically reporting a user without cause may result in sanctions
• Use this function responsibly

6.3. Response Time:
• We review reports within 24-48 hours
• Urgent cases (threats, illegal content) are prioritized
• You will receive notification of your report's outcome

7. Consequences for Violations

Violations of this policy may result in the following measures, depending on severity:

7.1. First Offense (Minor):
• Formal warning via email
• Removal of inappropriate content
• Policy reminder

7.2. Repeated or Moderate Offense:
• Temporary suspension (7-30 days)
• Restriction of functionalities (alert sending, comments)
• Reputation reduction
• Manual review of future content

7.3. Serious Offense:
• Extended suspension (30-90 days)
• Removal of all paid slots without refund
• Ban on creating new accounts
• Report to authorities if applicable

7.4. Very Serious Offense or Recidivism:
• Permanent account termination
• IP and device ban
• Loss of all data and content
• No possibility of refund
• Possible legal action
• Inclusion in blocked users list

7.5. Infractions Resulting in Immediate Expulsion:
• Illegal content (child pornography, terrorism)
• Serious threats or incitement to violence
• Documented fraud or identity theft
• Security attacks against the platform
• Sharing minors' data
• Recidivism after multiple suspensions

8. Appeals

If you believe a sanction was unfair:

• You can appeal by sending email to: appeals@warnapp.com
• Include your user ID and sanction details
• Provide evidence supporting your appeal
• Appeal deadline: 14 days from notification
• Response within 7 business days
• The appeal decision is final

Appeals are not accepted for:
• Clear illegal content
• Documented recidivism
• Security violations

9. Collaboration with Authorities

In cases of illegal or dangerous activity:

• We fully cooperate with competent authorities
• We provide data when legally required
• We proactively report serious crimes
• We preserve evidence for investigations

If you are a victim of a crime through the platform, immediately contact local authorities.

10. Modifications to this Policy

We may update this Acceptable Use Policy at any time. Changes will take effect immediately upon publication. Your continued use of the service constitutes acceptance of the new policies. Review this document periodically.

11. Contact

For inquiries about this policy:

Email: compliance@warnapp.com
Urgent reports: abuse@warnapp.com
Appeals: appeals@warnapp.com

Thank you for helping us keep WarnApp safe and useful for everyone.
